Science needs journalism!

Accurate Science journalism is an important job. Good scientific journalism brings focus to important work and connects people: scientists from different institutions, companies with academic collaborators, potential students to their future supervisors. For scientists, having work reported in the media can be an important identifier of real world impact and can help them understand the implications of their research, to plan ahead and to acquire funding. Maintaining high standards for scientific journalism and fighting back at fake news will help the scientific community in the long term.

Linking Science to News

Scientific research papers usually reference each other formally, facilitating a transparent acknowledgement and attribution of new ideas to the original authors. Journalism has no such system, meaning that it can be very difficult to understand which particular study, or even which particular academic a news article refers to. We are building a dataset of news stories linked to the scientific works that they discuss as a first step towards an automated system that will help keep scientific journalism transparent and help scientists to understand where they're having impact.

How does it work?

HarriGT provides a set of tooling that, given a news article, will suggest a set of scientific papers that could be relevant given the article metadata. The user's role is to determine whether any of these candidate papers is inherently linked to the news article and to file away news that has no relevant work associated with it and articles that are not about scientific topics.
